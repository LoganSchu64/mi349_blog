My Take on the Detroit Tigers

Logan Schuler 9/28/2025

Urban gardening has become a popular hobby for city dwellers seeking a touch of nature in their daily lives. Whether you have a small balcony, a rooftop, or even just a sunny windowsill, there are countless ways to grow your own food and flowers in the heart of the city. The benefits go beyond just fresh produce; gardening can reduce stress, improve air quality, and foster a sense of community among neighbors.

One of the most rewarding aspects of urban gardening is the ability to experiment with different plants and growing techniques. Container gardening, vertical gardens, and hydroponics are all excellent options for maximizing limited space. Many urban gardeners also participate in community gardens, sharing resources and knowledge while cultivating a shared green space.

If you’re new to urban gardening, start small. Choose easy-to-grow herbs like basil, mint, or chives, and gradually expand your garden as you gain confidence. Remember, every little bit of greenery helps make our cities more vibrant and sustainable!

Exploring the World of Indie Video Games

Logan Schuler 9/28/2025

The indie video game scene has exploded in recent years, offering players unique experiences that often break away from the conventions of mainstream gaming. Indie developers, unburdened by large corporate expectations, are free to experiment with new mechanics, art styles, and storytelling techniques. This creative freedom has led to the rise of some of the most beloved and innovative games of the past decade.

From pixel art adventures to emotionally charged narratives, indie games have something for everyone. Titles like "Celeste," "Hollow Knight," and "Stardew Valley" have proven that small teams—or even solo developers—can create masterpieces that rival big-budget productions. The accessibility of game development tools and digital distribution platforms has empowered more people than ever to bring their visions to life.

If you’re interested in exploring indie games, start by browsing online marketplaces or checking out recommendations from gaming communities. You might just discover your next favorite game—and support a passionate creator in the process!
